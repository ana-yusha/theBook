The Book of Yusha
Part 1: The Sin and Meeting

Preface:

This multi part series is to describe the life that we all wish to lead. The attempt to answer questions we all wish we could have answered. The topics in this series will make you question your faith; depending on if you even have faith, and it will give you a better faith in God if you are willing to complete the series. It will direct you to look inwardly and to use your heart to find God.

The very fact you are reading this book shows that you are trying to search out and understand God in a better way. You are trying to find the answers you so long for. These answers are often clouded by our judgment, logic and reason. You will not be able to use these techniques to understand God.

Many will not make it past this preface. If you are willing to open your mind, and more importantly as you will see, your heart. You will notice a change in your life that will be unexplainable. The only explanation for your heart changing is that you are being woken up and beginning to understand who God is.

The topics and stories will teach you how to search Him out on your own using various passages, parables, and research of past works.  We will cover topics from various major religions; Islam, Christianity and Jeudaism. Two of these “religions'' are less about religion and more so a deed given to man by various prophets. The other has taken a man who was given great responsibility by God and the human race turned him into God.

This story is not to batter and beat up any individuals or any religion in particular. We each have our own fears, doubts, and strife that occur to us day-by-day and we are unable to accept why such fate would befall us. Anything that happens on this Earth is by the Will of God and He is trying to teach you the best way He can from afar.

One of the main issues in our lives is that we do not spend enough time trying to help lift each other up out of the darkness. We would rather demean each other rather than worship Him directly with open arms and true submission to His will.

“So when they continued asking him, he lifted up himself, and said unto them, He that is without sin among you, let him cast a stone at her.” (John 8:7)

“And why do you see the speck that is in your brother’s eye, but do not notice the beam of wood in your own eye?” (Matthew 7:3)

The Sin:

To begin any sin is as detrimental to your soul as any other. There is no need to rehash the 10 covenants, the golden rule or the seven deadly sins, however, we will discuss these various topics and cover a more in depth description and explanation of these as we proceed through the story.

Growing up I was told that Jesus was the life, the blood and the only way to God. Raised as a devout Catholic I struggled to settle two differences within the story of Jesus and the first two Covenants . First, thou shall have no other Gods before me and secondly, thou shall not create any graven images and bow down to them.

It is impossible to reconcile the first two Covenants while elevating a man even one so wise and powerful as Jesus (Isa in Islam) to the stature of God. I was never able to reconcile these differences. Especially the way the Catholic and Christian faiths create images of a man on a cross with wounds bleeding from his heart or his side. Not only do we create these images even after being told not to even create them, we choose to bow before them.

By the age of thirteen I had knowingly, truly committed the first sin of my life. I had taken the confidence and trust bestowed upon me from a dear family member and destroyed that relationship. Our guardians told lies to settle the fear and uncertainty of the act. Though in their best efforts these lies only further stopped the healing process. We were never able to reconcile after such fear was placed over our relationship.

It is said that, “And if thy right hand should bring thee occasion to stumble, cut it off, and cast it from thee: for it is better for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell.” (Matthew 5:29-30). This passage brings us to one of the most difficult subjects we will cover in the series.

It is here that I would like to preface what I’m about to say; it will be difficult to hear, believe or even understand for numerous reasons. I am giving this message as an example and the truth of my life. I want to warn those against trying to repeat anything they read from this point forward. Let me be very clear, DO NOT try this at home.

I had searched all of the texts I could find to try and salvage my soul. I searched with all my heart to find the answer that I was looking for; I have sinned, how do I not go to hell?

Knowing as I did that Jesus was not God and that simply asking him for forgiveness was not going to save my soul. I took the aforementioned passage quite literally. I went down into the laundry room where my parents kept razor blades. I grabbed a blade and I returned to my bedroom and locked the door. I crept over to my closet and turned on the light.

I knelt in every direction, I prayed to every name I could find for the name of God. I asked that he hear me, I asked that he tell me the truth of this world. I begged and pleaded, crying all the while. As the hours passed I continued to contemplate removing his ability to work on my heart and give me the answer over time. I fell victim to another spell that this world has pushed upon us; instant gratification.

Some of the events of the evening are still a little foggy as it has been approximately 24 years since that night. I had mimicked what I was contemplating but it was more like a practice routine and an attempt to allow God to intervene.

By about the third round of doing this ritual, if you will; praying, sobbing, bowing and worshiping; trying to understand how to save my life after my death and mimicking splitting my arm open with the blade; it was then that I chose in that moment to complete the act.

As I closed my eyes; I did exactly what I thought the idea of Jesus would do at that moment. I vowed to Allah, YWHW, Elohim, Adonia that he could put the sins of the world past, present and future on my soul. I would be Jesus if he let me. How is it that he would allow so many lies to be told on the earth that no one could recognize who He was. It was unfair to the world in my opinion at the time that He would allow things to become so confusing with so many different religions that it was nearly impossible to know the truth. I grabbed the blade and split my arm from wrist to elbow as deep as I could.

 I felt the blood covering my arm and an immense pain in my left upper arm. I continued to tell myself it would be okay, it would be over soon. I felt my life force draining from my body and fell from my kneeling position down to the floor. Before my head hit the ground I was out. I couldn’t tell you how much time had passed before I heard the voice.

The smell was the worst smell I could imagine. Words could not describe the horrendous smell. I have not ever been around a decomposing body but I imagine that this smell would be worse than even that. I seemed lost, not being able to feel my body and was trying to get my bearings, however, nothing seemed to be right. As the time passed my mind began to panic asking am I dead, where am I, what is going on? The earliest parts of that conversation are the ones that are most unclear.

I was asking those questions about where I am, am I going to go to hell or heaven, and I faintly remember the voice telling me that I could go there if I wanted to. Thus we enter the meeting.

The Meeting:

I will not be covering every aspect of the conversation from that night in this chapter as it will be covered in the series over many chapters. I remember the voice told me that I was okay. I began to question the voice that I was hearing. Are you God…Are you Lucifer…Are you the Grim Reaper. I don’t know if it was at this exact moment but I eventually even questioned if he was like a genie and able to grant me three wishes. This idea will become more relevant later in this chapter.

The voice eventually told me of two names. Allah and Lucifer; being a Catholic and raised in America; such a name threw my soul for an even worse loop. Allah, how could that be, for as certain as my heart was that a God existed I was even more confused as to how all the stories I had learned could be a lie.

The voice was powerful, commanding, and loud. One which I cannot describe other than that of man. I asked the voice to answer the questions that I had. To show me the truth of this world. I had asked if the voice was Jesus and if he in fact was God on this Earth. He was very angered by the name Jesus and stated to not use it in His presence. His words were short, sweet and definitely not a direct answer. Many times I asked a question and no response was given. After some time I came to realize no answer was actually an answer because in my heart I knew the truth.

I had asked if he could resurrect me and allow me to live a life similar to Jesus, praising Allah’s name and telling anyone I came across the truth of Allah. Instead of asking for him to give me powers of healing I asked that he give me the power of foresight. So that I may tell people of the future and that they would understand the truth in my words in seeing it for themselves.

Since I knew making a mockery of the encounter would be displeasing to God. I asked that he allow me to make other changes in the world. Some may remember the passing of Mandela, people have this memory because he did at one time pass away in a prison, a horrible man. Many do not believe this memory or do not share this memory because they were Godless or for whatever reason Allah allowed and forced some to not remember.

Many of the changes people remember are not false memories as much as some would want you to believe. Names of companies and brands such as Berenstain bears or originally Berenstein bears and Jiffy becoming Jif; this was supposed to be meant as a sign to people in order to make them aware of God’s presence. As we all are aware He even changed a man who had died in prison and turned him into a figure of redemption.

As I made up this future full of different events I had asked him early on in our conversation to use my own voice because His voice was very strong and superior to my own. At this point I also unknowingly at the time, made it impossible to know if he was speaking to me or if I was being allowed to change the course of history and the future. We spoke of many things that were to have taken place in the past and things that would take place in the future.

Songs, movies and words for books and poems were given to people that are far better singers, musicians, artists, writers, actors and directors than I am. Many recent and even historical events that came from this conversation have a much deeper meaning to the story and they will be covered in the next chapters.  

I had asked him to give me a wife, two sons and a girl. We discussed their names which will not be revealed. Neither will any name of an individual whose experience I will share. These people deserve to live a private life and not have their lives affected by this message. I have since met and married the women that I asked for. We have since had two boys and one day in the not so distant future I pray that we are blessed with our girl.

Returning to our conversation, everything we talked about that night either has come to pass or will pass in the future. Thus bringing me to why you are reading this story. God does not choose his prophets, God does not choose who follows him. We each are able to seek out God with our own hearts. We each have His laws written on our hearts. We as individuals must choose God on our own for him to be able to set our paths straight. Even those who choose God will have difficulties in their lives that they must overcome. With His help and guidance He will give the way out of your torment.

“There hath no temptation taken you but such as is common to man: but God is faithful, who will not suffer you to be tempted above that ye are able; but will with the temptation also make a way to escape, that ye may be able to bear it”. (1 Corinthians 10:13)

The beating of your heart, the anxiety and thrill of sinning is very difficult to overcome. Many fall victim to our brains and our desires rather than following the heart which is clearly telling us we are about to sin and do something wrong. By telling you about Isa I am not saying that your soul is condemned even as you pray and continue to commit sin. You should do more good deeds than bad. We each will be judged according to Allah’s Will.

To fast forward a little it was as if my future life flashed before my eyes. Allah has given me great knowledge about the coming wars we are seeing today, the wars we saw in recent years. The warnings of a pandemic and an increase in disease and pestilence. Even famine as we are seeing and will continue to worsen. These topics will be covered in even greater detail in forthcoming chapters.

To close this chapter I must confess that I am not the messiah and this is not a new religion. Nothing was supposed to be a religion, it was supposed to be good men and women teaching and giving his works by example. I am merely a messenger of Allah. We as a world need to combat our fears and the internal strife we face. We need to turn our hearts to Allah and recognise that this world can only be saved in its current form by submitting to His Will.

At a point I realized my body was still in my room, whether he resurrected me or similar to the story of Ismael and Abraham. He allowed me to go to the edge and allowed me to think that I had in fact killed myself. I have no scar of the event, I have no proof of the conversation. I have been telling people this story everywhere I go and giving signs of the future so that they may know the truth. If these people still do not believe in God after the facts and evidence was presented to them. I cannot quell the fear within, I cannot give you the peace you are looking for.

I can merely point you in a direction and hope that you search your heart and the truth for yourself. You must choose God on your own and he will change your heart beyond your wildest imagination. Here I want to introduce a topic that many will not like to hear.

God truly does have many names and they are listed below for your reflection. You should learn these names and put them into practice in your everyday life. There is however, one name that is not in this list. Isa was trying to explain this concept to his disciples and the gentiles. This is the true reason Isa was killed.

I reference the Bible because the message is mostly pure. A few small changes have occurred during different translations, different manuscripts that were found and even many of the books have been burned by various groups throughout time. The templars, rome and other historical groups were so afraid of the 100th name of Allah. That name is Ana (Grace). You can become Ana if you truly with good intent search out God, ask for His forgiveness and trust that you will find the truth of this world and the next.

There are two points of reference I will leave with you to conclude this chapter. You can find Jesus using the phrase I am in multiple places in the bible. However, he was not expressing the meaning that people thought he was; that he was God. The reason Ana was converted into I am rather than Grace is due to Latin.

It means that you yourself can become like God, you do not need a priest, a pope, or any other “religious figure” to teach yourself who God is. The reason why this lie has persuaded so many and persevered over the centuries is because of the control it gives to those in power. It strips you of your ability to learn who God truly is and how you choosing him can open doors you couldn’t imagine.

“Jesus said unto her, I am (Ana/Grace) the resurrection, and life: he that believeth in me (Ana/Grace), though he were dead, yet shall he live:” (John 11:25)

“And whosoever liveth and believeth in me (Ana/Grace) shall never die. Believest thou this? (John 11:26)

I will leave you with a passage that sums this up from another person and is best described here:

This is a deep subject that is not really well-suited to a discussion on the internet, but here goes.

The 100th name of Allah is “ana” (me/I am). The learned scholar and mystic Mansur Al-Hallaj was put to death in the 9th century for revealing this secret. Many at the time did not understand the true meaning behind his words, “Ana 'l-Ḥaqq” (I am the truth), and those that did could see it was potentially dangerous to their prestige and authority. That’s why Hallaj had to be silenced.

Al Hallaj said:

I saw my Lord with the eye of the heart

I asked, 'Who are You?'

He replied, 'You'

It takes only a wise person to understand what was meant by this. Hallaj was saying that god is found within each of us, and that connecting with the divine takes effort, but is possible by contemplation and worship. Essentially, you can reach god within your heart - but only if you have pure and good niya (intent) and a good understanding of the Quran and the Sunnah and above all the true purpose of Islam, which is to set us free.

It’s not something that can be readily explained over the internet. Even learned scholars, who spent their lives studying Islam, know that matters are complex. Those who give quick, easy answers are those who do not know.

But that’s basically it.

People were killed over the centuries for knowledge of this. Because leaders and the powerful would not want ordinary people to know that they can think for themselves or find the truth by searching inwardly. Instead, leaders and clerics demand only subservient obedience to their demands. Any teaching that undermines obedience would only stand to weaken the power and influence of the mighty, which is why this truth was suppressed and hidden for so long.

Wa Allahu A’alam.

So you can see how Grace was replaced with ego, I am, me and the incorrect understanding taught about the bible and Jesus for centuries.

Peace be upon you all, and may your sins be forgiven.
